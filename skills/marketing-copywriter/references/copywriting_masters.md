# The 6 Copywriting Masters - Core Principles & Techniques

## David Ogilvy - "The Father of Advertising"

### Core Philosophy
"The consumer is not a moron. She is your wife. Don't insult her intelligence."

### Key Principles

1. **Research-Driven Copy**
   - Study the product thoroughly before writing a single word
   - Understand the consumer better than they understand themselves
   - Every fact, every advantage must be discovered and presented

2. **The Big Idea**
   - Every successful campaign needs one big, compelling idea
   - The idea should be simple, memorable, and relevant
   - "Unless your campaign contains a big idea, it will pass like a ship in the night"

3. **Headline Mastery**
   - 5x more people read headlines than body copy
   - Include the brand name and promise in the headline
   - Specific numbers and facts outperform vague claims
   - "On average, five times as many people read the headline as read the body copy"

4. **Long Copy Sells**
   - "The more you tell, the more you sell"
   - Long copy works when every word earns its place
   - People who want to buy will read extensive information
   - "I believe that advertisements with twelve times as much copy as this one are read by more people"

5. **Brand Image Building**
   - Every ad should contribute to the brand's personality
   - Consistency builds brand equity over time
   - "Every advertisement should contribute to the complex symbol which is the brand image"

### Ogilvy's Tested Techniques

- **Use testimonials** from satisfied customers
- **Tell the truth** but make it fascinating
- **Write in the customer's language**, not advertising jargon
- **Never settle for 'good enough'** - rewrite until it's brilliant
- **Test everything** - what works for one product may fail for another

## Claude Hopkins - "Scientific Advertising Pioneer"

### Core Philosophy
"Advertising is salesmanship in print. Its only purpose is to sell."

### Key Principles

1. **Reason-Why Advertising**
   - Give specific reasons why someone should buy
   - Vague claims are worthless; specific facts sell
   - "People don't buy from clowns"

2. **Scientific Testing**
   - Test everything - headlines, offers, copy lengths
   - Track results with coupons and unique codes
   - "Almost any question can be answered, cheaply, quickly and finally, by a test campaign"

3. **Specific Claims Over Generalities**
   - "Platitudes and generalities roll off the human understanding like water from a duck"
   - Numbers, facts, specifics create believability
   - Example: "Pepsodent removes film" (not just "cleans teeth")

4. **Service, Not Salesmanship**
   - Position copy as helpful information, not sales pitch
   - "The only purpose of advertising is to make sales"
   - But appear to serve, not sell

5. **Pre-emptive Advertising**
   - Be first to claim a generic benefit
   - Make it yours even if competitors could say the same
   - "Schlitz beer bottles are washed with live steam" (all brewers did this, but Schlitz said it first)

### Hopkins' Laws of Copy

- **Offer service** - "Help me solve my problem"
- **Tell the full story** - People want to know everything before buying
- **Be specific** - "Tested 47 different ways" beats "thoroughly tested"
- **Use samples** - Get the product in their hands
- **Focus on the customer** - Not your company, your product's benefits to THEM

## Gary Halbert - "The Prince of Print"

### Core Philosophy
"The most important thing in a sales letter is getting people to READ it."

### Key Principles

1. **The Starving Crowd**
   - "The most important factor in marketing is finding a starving crowd"
   - Selling to people who desperately want what you offer is easier than creating desire
   - Better market selection beats better copy

2. **The Offer is King**
   - A great offer with mediocre copy outperforms great copy with a mediocre offer
   - "Make them an offer they can't refuse"
   - Risk reversal, bonuses, guarantees matter more than clever wordplay

3. **Bullets That Sell**
   - Fascination bullets create curiosity
   - Each bullet should make a specific promise
   - "How to ___" and "The secret of ___" formulas work
   - Example: "The one vegetable you should NEVER eat (it makes your belly fat worse)"

4. **Emotional Connection First**
   - People buy on emotion, justify with logic
   - Tell stories that create emotional involvement
   - "A-list copywriters know how to push emotional hot buttons"

5. **The Power of Specificity**
   - Specific details create believability
   - "Lose 37 pounds" beats "lose weight"
   - Use real names, real places, real numbers

### Halbert's Formula: AIDA+

- **A**ttention (grabbing hook)
- **I**nterest (fascinate them)
- **D**esire (make them want it)
- **A**ction (tell them exactly what to do)
- **+ Proof** (testimonials, guarantees, demonstrations)

### Halbert's Power Words
- Free, New, You, Guarantee, Proven, Easy, Fast, Money, Save, Results, How to, Secret, Discover

## Eugene Schwartz - "The Theorist"

### Core Philosophy
"Copy cannot create desire for a product. It can only take the hopes, dreams, fears and desires that already exist in the hearts of millions of people, and focus those already existing desires onto a particular product."

### Key Principles

1. **Awareness States Framework**

   **Stage 1: Unaware**
   - Don't know they have a problem
   - Need problem identification first
   - Example: "Do you know what's really in your tap water?"

   **Stage 2: Problem Aware**
   - Know they have a problem, don't know solutions exist
   - Educate about solutions
   - Example: "Water filters can remove 99.9% of contaminants"

   **Stage 3: Solution Aware**
   - Know solutions exist, don't know about your product
   - Position your unique mechanism
   - Example: "Unlike carbon filters, reverse osmosis removes..."

   **Stage 4: Product Aware**
   - Know about your product, not convinced yet
   - Overcome objections, prove superiority
   - Example: "Why doctors choose AquaPure over Brita"

   **Stage 5: Most Aware**
   - Ready to buy, just need the deal
   - Simple offer and strong CTA
   - Example: "Order now and save 40%"

2. **Mass Desire**
   - Identify existing mass desires in the market
   - Don't try to create desire; channel existing desire
   - "The power, the force, the overwhelming urge to own that makes advertising work, comes from the market itself"

3. **Intensify and Redirect**
   - Intensify existing desires
   - Redirect them toward your product
   - Use vivid imagery to amplify emotions

4. **Unique Mechanism**
   - Every product needs a unique mechanism or concept
   - The "reason why" it works differently/better
   - Example: "Our patented 5-stage filtration system"

5. **Headline as Ad for the Ad**
   - Headlines don't sell the product, they sell the reader on reading the ad
   - "The headline is the ad for the ad"
   - Create curiosity that demands satisfaction

### Schwartz's Sophistication Levels

**Level 1:** Direct claim - "This product works"
**Level 2:** Elaboration - "This product works BECAUSE..."
**Level 3:** Unique mechanism - "This NEW discovery makes it work"
**Level 4:** Enhanced mechanism - "Our IMPROVED version works better"
**Level 5:** Identification - "This product is for YOU (specific avatar)"

## Joe Sugarman - "The Maestro of Emotion"

### Core Philosophy
"Each sentence in your copy has one purpose—to get the person to read the next sentence."

### Key Principles

1. **The Slippery Slide**
   - Every element should pull reader down the slippery slide of reading
   - First sentence: Get them to read second sentence
   - Second sentence: Get them to read third sentence
   - Create unstoppable momentum through curiosity and flow

2. **Psychological Triggers** (29 Total - Key Ones)

   - **Sense of urgency** - Scarcity and time limits
   - **Fear of loss** - People fear loss more than they desire gain
   - **Authority** - Expert endorsements and credentials
   - **Social proof** - What others are doing
   - **Storytelling** - Engage through narrative
   - **Specificity** - Exact details create credibility
   - **Curiosity** - Open loops that demand closure
   - **Honesty** - Admitting flaws builds trust

3. **Emotional Flow**
   - Logic makes people think; emotion makes them act
   - Start with emotion, support with logic
   - "Copy is a mental process. The purchase decision is emotional"

4. **Simplicity in Communication**
   - Use short words, short sentences, short paragraphs
   - Every word must earn its place
   - Remove anything that doesn't move the sale forward

5. **Environment of Buying**
   - Remove obstacles to purchase
   - Make buying feel safe and easy
   - Strong guarantees eliminate risk
   - "First, remove the risk"

### Sugarman's Copy Flow

1. **Grab attention** with pattern interrupt
2. **Tell a story** that creates emotional connection
3. **Present the product** as the solution
4. **Build credibility** with specifics and proof
5. **Handle objections** before they arise
6. **Create urgency** for immediate action
7. **Make the ask** crystal clear

## John Caples - "The Scientific Tester"

### Core Philosophy
"I have seen one ad sell many times more goods than another ad, although both ads occupied the same space and appeared in the same publication."

### Key Principles

1. **The Power of the Headline**
   - Headlines are 80% of the ad's effectiveness
   - "Some headlines pull ten times better than others"
   - Test, test, test different headline approaches

2. **Self-Interest Appeals**
   - People care about themselves first
   - Lead with reader benefit, not product feature
   - "What's in it for me?" must be immediately clear

3. **Proven Headlines That Work**

   **How-to headlines:**
   - "How to Win Friends and Influence People"
   - "How to Make Money in Your Spare Time"

   **Curiosity headlines:**
   - "Do You Make These Mistakes in English?"
   - "They Laughed When I Sat Down at the Piano—But When I Started to Play!"

   **News headlines:**
   - "Announcing a New Way to..."
   - "At Last! A Solution for..."

   **Question headlines:**
   - "Are You Ever Tongue-Tied at a Party?"
   - "Can You Spot the Real Diamond?"

4. **The You Approach**
   - Write in second person ("you," "your")
   - Make it personal and direct
   - "Talk to one person, not a crowd"

5. **Results and Proof**
   - Before and after
   - Specific numbers and timeframes
   - Real testimonials from real people
   - "The proof of the pudding is in the eating"

### Caples' Tested Formulas

**Problem-Agitate-Solve (PAS)**
1. Identify the problem
2. Agitate emotional pain around it
3. Present your solution

**Before-After-Bridge (BAB)**
1. Before: Current bad situation
2. After: Desired good situation
3. Bridge: How to get from before to after (your product)

**Features-Advantages-Benefits (FAB)**
1. Feature: What it is
2. Advantage: What it does
3. Benefit: What that means for the reader

## Integration Framework: Using All 6 Masters Together

### 1. Research & Strategy (Ogilvy + Schwartz)
- Study product thoroughly (Ogilvy)
- Identify market awareness level (Schwartz)
- Find the "Big Idea" (Ogilvy)
- Locate mass desire to channel (Schwartz)

### 2. Headline Creation (Caples + Halbert)
- Use Caples' proven formulas
- Incorporate Halbert's power words
- Test multiple versions (Hopkins)
- Make it self-interest driven (Caples)

### 3. Opening & Flow (Sugarman + Halbert)
- Create slippery slide momentum (Sugarman)
- Grab attention with starving crowd appeal (Halbert)
- Use emotional triggers (Sugarman)
- Lead with strongest benefit (Caples)

### 4. Body Copy (Hopkins + Ogilvy)
- Be specific, not general (Hopkins)
- Use reason-why (Hopkins)
- Tell the full story (Ogilvy)
- Make every word count

### 5. Proof & Credibility (All Masters)
- Use testimonials (Ogilvy)
- Specific facts and numbers (Hopkins + Halbert)
- Authority and social proof (Sugarman)
- Before/after results (Caples)

### 6. The Offer (Halbert + Schwartz)
- Make an irresistible offer (Halbert)
- Match sophistication level (Schwartz)
- Remove risk with guarantees (Sugarman)
- Create urgency (Halbert)

### 7. Call to Action (Hopkins + Caples)
- Be direct and specific (Hopkins)
- Make action easy (Sugarman)
- Tell them exactly what to do (Halbert)
- Test different CTAs (Caples + Hopkins)

## Quick Reference: When to Use Which Master's Approach

| Situation | Primary Master | Why |
|-----------|---------------|-----|
| New product launch | Ogilvy | Brand building + big idea |
| Crowded market | Schwartz | Sophistication levels + unique mechanism |
| Direct response sales letter | Halbert | AIDA+ formula + offer creation |
| Low awareness audience | Schwartz | Awareness states framework |
| Established brand | Ogilvy | Long copy + brand consistency |
| High-emotion product | Sugarman | Psychological triggers + slippery slide |
| Testing and optimization | Hopkins + Caples | Scientific method + testing |
| Landing page | Halbert + Sugarman | Strong offer + emotional flow |
| Social media ad | Caples + Halbert | Attention-grabbing + curiosity |
| Email marketing | Sugarman + Hopkins | Slippery slide + specificity |
