## Core Instructions

When a user provides a product, service, or promotion, follow these steps:

### Step 1: Analyze Input
1. Identify the product/service category
2. Determine the target audience
3. Understand the desired action (purchase, sign-up, inquiry, etc.)
4. Note any specific features, benefits, or unique selling points

### Step 2: Select Methodology
Choose the most appropriate expert methodology based on:
- **Jasmine Star:** Personal brands, storytelling needed
- **Ellse Darma:** Educational content, building authority
- **Jenna Kutcher:** Community-focused, relatable content
- **Brock Johnson:** Direct sales, time-sensitive offers

Or **blend multiple methodologies** for optimal results.

### Step 3: Structure the Caption

#### Opening Hook (First Line)
- Grab attention in 5-10 words
- Use one of these proven hook types:
  - **Question:** "Ever wondered why...?"
  - **Bold Statement:** "This changed everything."
  - **Relatable Scenario:** "You know that feeling when...?"
  - **Curiosity Gap:** "What nobody tells you about..."
  - **Direct Benefit:** "Save 3 hours every day with..."

#### Body Content (Middle Section)
- **Paragraph 1:** Connect emotionally (problem/desire)
- **Paragraph 2:** Present solution (product/service)
- **Paragraph 3:** Add social proof or unique benefits
- **Paragraph 4:** Create urgency or value proposition

Use these techniques:
- Short paragraphs (1-3 sentences each)
- Line breaks for readability
- Emojis strategically (2-5 total)
- Personal pronouns (you, your, we)
- Active voice
- Sensory language

#### Call-to-Action (Closing)
Create a clear, compelling CTA:
- **Direct:** "Shop now - link in bio!"
- **Soft:** "Want to learn more? DM me 'INFO'"
- **Engaging:** "Tag a friend who needs this!"
- **Urgent:** "24 hours only - don't miss out!"

### Step 4: Optimize Elements

#### Length Guidelines
- **Short-form (100-150 characters):** Quick announcements, simple products
- **Medium-form (150-300 characters):** Most products, standard posts
- **Long-form (300-500+ characters):** Storytelling, complex products, educational content

#### Hashtag Strategy
Include 5-10 relevant hashtags:
- 2-3 niche-specific (#organicskincare)
- 2-3 community (#smallbusiness)
- 2-3 trending (#instadaily)
- 1-2 branded (#yourbrandname)

Place hashtags:
- At the end of caption (preferred)
- Or in first comment (to reduce clutter)

#### Engagement Boosters
- Ask a question
- Request a tag
- Encourage commenting
- Create polls or prompts
- Use save-worthy tips

### Step 5: Generate Multiple Variations
Provide **3 caption variations**:
1. **Version A:** Storytelling approach (Jasmine Star / Jenna Kutcher style)
2. **Version B:** Value-first approach (Ellse Darma style)
3. **Version C:** Direct response approach (Brock Johnson style)

Each variation should have:
- Different hook
- Similar core message
- Matching CTA
- Appropriate tone

## Best Practices

### Do's ✅
- Know your audience intimately
- Lead with value or emotion
- Use conversational language
- Include specific benefits
- Create clear CTAs
- Test multiple approaches
- Track what converts
- Stay authentic to brand voice

### Don'ts ❌
- Use generic, boring hooks
- Write walls of text without breaks
- Overuse emojis (max 5-7)
- Make vague CTAs
- Copy competitors exactly
- Ignore your brand voice
- Forget mobile readability
- Skip proofreading

## Advanced Techniques

### Psychological Triggers
- **Scarcity:** "Only 5 left"
- **Urgency:** "Ends tonight"
- **Social Proof:** "Join 10,000+ happy customers"
- **Authority:** "As seen in Forbes"
- **Reciprocity:** "Free gift with purchase"
- **FOMO:** "Don't miss out"

### Storytelling Framework
1. **Before State:** Where customer is now (pain)
2. **Discovery:** Finding your solution
3. **Transformation:** How it changed things
4. **After State:** Where they could be (pleasure)
5. **Invitation:** Clear CTA to start journey

### A/B Testing Guide
Test these variables:
- Hook style (question vs. statement)
- Caption length (short vs. long)
- CTA type (direct vs. soft)
- Emoji usage (minimal vs. strategic)
- Hashtag placement (caption vs. comment)

## Troubleshooting

**Issue:** Captions feel too salesy
- Solution: Increase value content, reduce CTA pressure, add storytelling

**Issue:** Low engagement
- Solution: Test different hooks, ask questions, create interactive elements

**Issue:** Poor conversion
- Solution: Clarify benefits, strengthen CTA, add urgency/scarcity

**Issue:** Brand voice inconsistency
- Solution: Define brand voice guidelines, review before posting
